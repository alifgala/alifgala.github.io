WHAT IS DATA SCIENCE?

Data science is an interdisciplinary field that involves mining large data sets of structured and unstructured data to identify patterns and extract actionable insights. It is based on statistics, inference, computer science, predictive analytics, machine learning algorithm development, and new technologies. The life cycle of data science involves capturing, maintaining, processing, and analyzing data. Data scientists stand out during data exploration and processing, which involves data mining, classification, clustering, modeling, and summarizing insights. Data analysis is crucial, involving exploratory and confirmatory work, regression, predictive analysis, qualitative analysis, and text mining. Data visualization, reporting, and the use of business intelligence tools are essential for communicating insights and assisting businesses, policymakers, and others in smarter decision-making.

WHAT DATA SCIENCE USED FOR?

Data science applications are frequently used in healthcare, marketing, banking and finance, and policy work. Here are some common examples of data science services in action in trending data science fields:

  • Transforming Healthcare using Data Science

    • Data science is changing healthcare as consumers and clinicians use wearable data to monitor and prevent health issues. A “big data revolution” in healthcare was described by McKinsey in 2018. McKinsey estimates that using data science to US healthcare may cut spending by $300 billion to $450 billion.

  • Data science in business

    • In business, data science and analytics combine. Data science helps firms understand client demands using existing data. Data scientists can better train search and product recommendation models with consumer age, purchase history, browsing history, income, and other factors.

  • Data Science in Finance

    • Data science improves financial companies' ability to spot suspicious data patterns and avoid fraud. Data science can reduce non-performing assets and reveal declines faster.

  • Policy Work Transformation with Data Science

    • Data science may help government leaders fulfill constituent requirements, battle census undercount with big data and ML, and more. Policymakers may use geospatial data science and associated data to determine evacuation times based on previous weather patterns. These decisions can be made using correlative or descriptive data analysis.
